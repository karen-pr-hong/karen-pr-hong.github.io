Algorithm training practice to predict user gender by tweet content

Algorithm training practice to predict user gender by tweet content

Apr 2019

R, Decision Tree

Twitter Text Analytics

The exercise uses open source dataset from Kaggle to conduct text analytics. I used the content of a random tweet to judge whether the user is a female or male. The original data set contains 20K rows, each with a user name, a random tweet, account profile and image, location, the sidebar color. My exercise simplify the dataset to 12K rows by excluding usernames that belongs to a brand (non-individual). Classification and Regression Tree (CART): when there is "game" or its stem words such as "gaming" in the tweet, predict the user is a male:


ACCURACY RATE 52.61%, HIGHER THAN BASELINE 51.96%


                  predictCART
                female   male
female     1994         16
male         1817         41

The accuracy rate of the CART model, (1994+41)/(1994+16+1817+41) = 52.61%, is higher than baseline rate, (1994+16)/(1994+16+1817+41) = 51.96%, when we predict all account users were female.

ESG Research on Taipei Exchange Listed Company

ESG Research on Taipei Exchange Listed Company

Mar 2016

ESG

ESG Research on Taipei Exchange Listed Companies as a Research Assistant at National Taipei Univeristy

I was part of the ESG research team at National Taipei University when I was an undergrad student. Our team was led by Professors with Sustainable Investment expertise and at that time sponsored by PineBridge Management to conduct ESG researches. We have researched company annual reports, Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) reports, information platform of Taipei Exchange. We evaluated the companies performance with hundreds of questions based on social, economic, environmental, and disclosure (SEED) dimensions. The research has continued and evolved throughout the years. Now they have colaborated with Solactive, a German-based financial indices provider, and launched the Taiwanese Sustainability Index.
